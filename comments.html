Simple wireframes on the right show the structure of the four HTML pages and what they look like. There are 3 cards with “Read More” buttons on the homepage. With these buttons, the users can move to other pages, or stick to the top navigation bar. On the other three pages, there are links to those using the top navigation bar.

As for colour scheme, I chose dark blue, dark green and orange with white. Dark tones to show your professionalism and your logical side, that is what an IT Degree student should be! But the orange one gives a bit of enthusiasm to also let you know, that I'm passionate also for IT. I adopted flat design for the design style that, I hope, can give readers a facile and nifty way to read and show the reader part of my personality, some experiences and something I want in the future.

alt tag is useful by presenting alternative text when website is not able to render a image. And regarding the css, removing it or disabling it would make the layout on the website a mess. But, the user could get about the pages by using the navigation bar. That being said, the site's aesthetics and user experience would take a huge hit. This proves the importance of CSS for every web.
